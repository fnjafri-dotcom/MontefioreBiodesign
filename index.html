Program Overview

A 12-month, project-based program taking clinician and trainee teams from an unmet clinical need to a tested minimum viable product (MVP), final pitch, and dissemination plan — using the Stanford Biodesign process (identify → invent → implement) augmented with AI tools at each stage.

DirectorsProgram leadership (Dr. Jariwala) — office hours by request
MeetsMonthly seminar (see each month's section for agenda) + team working sessions
Teams3–4 members per team, with faculty mentor(s) identified in Month 1
Final deliverablesTested MVP · End-of-year pitch presentation · Abstract or manuscript · IP/sustainability plan

What You Will Be Able to Do by June

1. Conduct a structured needs assessment and select a validated, high-impact clinical need.
2. Perform an AI-enabled market assessment (TAM/SAM/SOM), segmentation, and competitive analysis (SWOT, Porter's Five Forces).
3. Build and pilot-test an MVP with a defined testing protocol and key performance indicators.
4. Deliver a compelling elevator pitch and investor-style pitch deck.
5. Navigate IRB, intellectual property disclosure, and dissemination (abstract/manuscript) pathways.

Month 1 — September 2026: Orientation, Teams & Needs Finding

Theme: "A good problem is worth a hundred solutions." This month is about setup and disciplined needs identification — not solutions yet.

Learning objectives — by the end of this month you can:

1. Explain the biodesign innovation cycle (identify → invent → implement) and where AI accelerates each phase.
2. Distinguish a well-characterized clinical need statement (population + problem + outcome) from a solution in disguise.
3. Apply Thiel's "zero to one" and Christensen's disruption frameworks to critique one existing digital health product.
4. Begin a literature and stakeholder discovery workflow using PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, Crunchbase, and FDA digital health reports.

Read / listen before the Month 1 session

  • Read Zero to One (Peter Thiel) — Ch. 1–4 this month; finish by end of Month 2.
  • Read The Innovator's Dilemma (Clay Christensen) — Introduction + Ch. 1; finish by end of Month 2.
  • Read NEJM Catalyst: The Clinician Role in Health Care Delivery and Innovation.
  • Listen Innovation Biodesign Podcast: "Digital Health Innovation and Biodesign."
